Popular places to visit

Chamera Lake

Chamera Lake, located in the Chamba district of Himachal Pradesh, India, is a stunning reservoir formed by the Chamera Dam on the Ravi River. Situated at an altitude of approximately 763 meters (2,503 feet) above sea level, the lake is surrounded by lush green valleys and towering mountains, making it a picturesque destination for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts. The lake serves as a major source of water for irrigation and drinking purposes in the region. Its calm and serene waters, coupled with the absence of aquatic life, make it an ideal spot for water sports such as boating, kayaking, and fishing. The Himachal Pradesh Tourism Department has established a water sports center near the lake to promote tourism and provide facilities for visitors. Chamera Lake is also a popular picnic spot, offering breathtaking views and a tranquil environment. The surrounding hills provide opportunities for trekking and photography, making it a favorite among adventure seekers. The lake's proximity to Dalhousie, located about 25 kilometers away, adds to its appeal as a day-trip destination. The best time to visit Chamera Lake is during the summer months, from March to June, when the weather is pleasant and the landscapes are vibrant. However, the lake remains accessible throughout the year, offering unique experiences in different seasons. Whether you're drawn by its natural beauty, adventure opportunities, or peaceful ambiance, Chamera Lake is a must-visit destination in Himachal Pradesh, providing a perfect blend of relaxation and exploration.

Khajjiar

Khajjiar, often referred to as the "Mini Switzerland of India," is a picturesque hill station located in the Chamba district of Himachal Pradesh. Nestled at an altitude of approximately 2,000 meters (6,500 feet) above sea level, Khajjiar is surrounded by dense deodar forests, lush green meadows, and snow-capped mountains, offering breathtaking views and a tranquil ambiance. The main attraction of Khajjiar is its vast grassy plateau, which features a small lake at its center. The lake is fed by natural springs and is surrounded by cedar trees, creating a serene and enchanting environment. The area is part of the Kalatop Khajjiar Wildlife Sanctuary, which is home to a variety of flora and fauna, making it a haven for nature lovers. Khajjiar is also known for its cultural and historical significance. The Khajji Nag Temple, dating back to the 12th century, is dedicated to the serpent god Khajji Nag and features intricate wood carvings and a blend of Hindu and Mughal architectural styles. The temple adds a spiritual dimension to the beauty of the region. Adventure enthusiasts can enjoy activities like paragliding, zorbing, and horse riding in Khajjiar. The hill station is easily accessible from Dalhousie, located about 24 kilometers away, and is a popular destination for day trips and weekend getaways. The best time to visit Khajjiar is from March to June and September to November, when the weather is pleasant and the landscapes are vibrant. Whether you're seeking tranquility, adventure, or cultural exploration, Khajjiar offers an unforgettable experience in the heart of Himachal Pradesh.

Bharmour

Bharmour, a picturesque town in the Chamba district of Himachal Pradesh, India, is steeped in history, spirituality, and natural beauty. Situated at an altitude of approximately 2,100 meters (6,900 feet) above sea level, Bharmour is surrounded by the majestic Pir Panjal and Dhauladhar mountain ranges, offering breathtaking views and a serene ambiance. Known as "Shiva Bhumi" or the land of Lord Shiva, Bharmour is home to the famous Chaurasi Temple complex, which features 84 ancient shrines believed to be over 1,400 years old. The temples are dedicated to various deities, with the Manimahesh Temple, devoted to Lord Shiva, being the most prominent. Bharmour serves as the base for the Manimahesh Yatra, an annual pilgrimage to the sacred Manimahesh Lake. The town is also known for its vibrant culture and traditions, with the Gaddi community being the primary inhabitants. Their unique lifestyle and customs add to the charm of the region. Bharmour's lush green meadows, terraced farms, and alpine pastures provide a tranquil setting for visitors. Adventure enthusiasts can explore trekking routes to nearby destinations, including Bharmani Devi Temple and Manimahesh Lake. The best time to visit Bharmour is from April to October, as the winter months bring heavy snowfall, making the region less accessible. Whether you're drawn by its spiritual significance, historical allure, or natural beauty, Bharmour offers a unique and unforgettable experience in the heart of Himachal Pradesh.
