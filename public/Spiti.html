Spiti Valley

Spiti Valley is a cold desert mountain region known for its remote landscapes and ancient monasteries. Tabo Monastery, over a thousand years old, holds historic Buddhist art and scriptures. Dhankar Lake, above the Dhankar Monastery, is a hidden gem offering peace and striking views. Chandra Taal, or Moon Lake, is a high-altitude lake with turquoise waters and surrounding snow-capped peaks, attracting trekkers and photographers for its surreal beauty and silence.

Popular places to visit

Chandrataal

Chandrataal, also known as the "Moon Lake," is a stunning high-altitude lake located in the Spiti Valley of Himachal Pradesh, India. Situated at an elevation of approximately 4,300 meters (14,100 feet) above sea level, the lake derives its name from its crescent shape, resembling a moon. It is surrounded by barren mountains on one side and lush meadows on the other, creating a striking contrast that captivates visitors. The lake is a popular destination for trekkers and nature enthusiasts. The trek to Chandrataal offers breathtaking views of the Himalayan landscape, including rugged valleys, glaciers, and vibrant wildflowers. The journey to the lake can be undertaken from Batal or Kunzum Pass, with the latter offering a more challenging but rewarding experience. The lake is accessible by road during the summer months, but the final stretch requires a short hike. Chandrataal is known for its crystal-clear waters that change hues throughout the day, ranging from turquoise to green and blue. The lake is surrounded by vast meadows, which serve as camping sites for visitors. During spring, these meadows are adorned with hundreds of varieties of wildflowers, adding to the lake's charm. The lake holds mythological significance, as it is believed to be the site where Lord Indra's chariot picked up Yudhishthira, the eldest Pandava, to take him to heaven in his mortal form. This legend adds a spiritual dimension to the lake's allure. The best time to visit Chandrataal is from June to September, when the weather is favorable and the roads are accessible. Whether you're drawn by its natural beauty, trekking opportunities, or spiritual significance, Chandrataal offers an unforgettable experience in the heart of the Himalayas.

Dhankar

Dhankar, a picturesque village in the Spiti Valley of Himachal Pradesh, India, is a destination that combines natural beauty, historical significance, and spiritual charm. Perched at an altitude of approximately 3,894 meters (12,774 feet), Dhankar offers breathtaking views of the confluence of the Spiti and Pin rivers, surrounded by rugged mountains and serene landscapes. The village is best known for the Dhankar Monastery, also called Dhankar Gompa, which is one of the oldest monasteries in India. Built over 1,000 years ago, the monastery is perched on a cliff, offering panoramic views of the valley below. The word "Dhankar" translates to "fort on a cliff," reflecting its strategic location. The monastery is a treasure trove of Buddhist art and culture, housing ancient murals, scriptures, and statues. Nearby, the new Dhankar Monastery continues to serve as a spiritual center for the local community. A short trek from the village leads to Dhankar Lake, a tranquil high-altitude lake surrounded by stunning scenery. The lake is a popular spot for trekkers and nature lovers, offering a peaceful retreat amidst the Himalayas. Dhankar was once the capital of the Spiti Valley and served as the seat of the Nono kings, adding historical significance to its charm. The best time to visit Dhankar is from May to October, as the winter months bring heavy snowfall, making the region less accessible. Whether you're drawn by its spiritual heritage, natural beauty, or historical allure, Dhankar offers a unique and unforgettable experience in the heart of the Spiti Valley. Tabo Village

Tabo Village, located in the Spiti Valley of Himachal Pradesh, India, is a serene and culturally rich destination. Situated at an altitude of approximately 3,280 meters (10,760 feet) above sea level, the village is nestled along the banks of the Spiti River and surrounded by stark, barren mountains. Known as the "Ajanta of the Himalayas," Tabo is home to the ancient Tabo Monastery, which is one of the oldest active Buddhist monasteries in India. Founded in 996 AD by the great scholar Richen Zangpo, the Tabo Monastery is renowned for its exquisite murals, frescoes, and stucco sculptures that depict tales from Mahayana Buddhism. The monastery complex includes nine temples, numerous stupas, monks' quarters, and meditation caves carved into the cliffs above the village. The serene atmosphere and historical significance of the monastery make it a spiritual and cultural treasure. Tabo Village serves as a pit stop for travelers exploring the Spiti Valley and nearby attractions like Dhankar Monastery, Pin Valley, and Gue Village. The village offers opportunities for trekking, photography, and stargazing, with its clear skies and dramatic landscapes providing a perfect backdrop. The best time to visit Tabo is from May to October, when the weather is favorable and the roads are accessible. Whether you're drawn by its spiritual heritage, historical allure, or natural beauty, Tabo Village offers a unique and unforgettable experience in the heart of the Himalayas.
